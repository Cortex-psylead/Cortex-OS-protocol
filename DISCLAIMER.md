# ⚠️ Disclaimer & Safety Notice: Cortex OS Protocol

**Read this document before using, implementing, or contributing
to any component of Cortex OS Protocol.**

---

## 🏥 Not a Medical Device

Cortex OS Protocol is a **research and development protocol**.
It is not a medical device, a clinical intervention tool, or a
substitute for professional medical or psychological judgment.

Specifically:

- The HRV monitoring module is **not** a diagnostic tool for
  cardiac conditions
- The autonomic regulation modules are **not** a treatment for
  any psychological or neurological disorder
- The neurodivergence support modules are **not** a substitute
  for clinical evaluation or therapeutic intervention
- The therapeutic audio modules are **not** a medical treatment
  for anxiety, depression, ADHD, ASD, or any other condition

If you are experiencing a mental health crisis or a medical
emergency, contact your local emergency services or a qualified
professional immediately.

**Global resources:**
- findahelpline.com — worldwide crisis line directory
- befrienders.org — emotional support worldwide

---

## 🔬 Academic and Research Purpose

At its current stage, Cortex OS Protocol is a **research framework**
with a strictly academic and developmental purpose.

- The clinical protocols described in CLINICAL-BRIDGE.md are based
  on peer-reviewed literature but have not been validated as
  implemented in this specific system
- No clinical trials have been conducted using Cortex OS components
- The physiological parameters described are reference values from
  published research — they require adaptation and validation for
  any specific implementation or population

Any academic or research use of this protocol must comply with
the ethical standards of the relevant institution, including
informed consent procedures and IRB/ethics committee approval
where applicable.

---

## ⚙️ Hardware Implementation Risk

Cortex OS Protocol interacts with low-level hardware components
including NPU, Ray Tracing cores, DSP, and Trusted Execution
Environment (TEE).

**By implementing any hardware component of this protocol, you
acknowledge that:**

- Hardware modification and kernel-level development carry inherent
  risks including device instability, data loss, and hardware damage
- Implementation on real hardware is the sole responsibility of
  the person or team executing it
- The project maintainers and Ethical Court nodes are not liable
  for any damage resulting from hardware implementation
- Testing on production devices without proper backup and recovery
  procedures is strongly discouraged

## 👤 User Responsibility

By using, forking, or building upon Cortex OS Protocol, you agree that:

- You are responsible for compliance with applicable laws and
  regulations in your jurisdiction
- You will not use this protocol to collect, process, or transmit
  sensitive health data without appropriate legal authorization
  and user consent
- You will not deploy clinical-adjacent modules (HRV, ANS regulation,
  neurodivergence support) in production environments without
  validation by qualified professionals and appropriate regulatory
  compliance
- You will clearly communicate to end users that Cortex OS is a
  research protocol, not a certified medical tool
